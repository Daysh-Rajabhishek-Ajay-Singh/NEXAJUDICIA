# NEXAJUDICIA
A Novel Transformer-XAI Framework with Bias Attribution and A-RAG for Indian Judicial System

NEXAJudicia is an advanced, bias-aware and interpretable AI framework for legal judgment prediction, designed to enhance transparency, fairness, and accountability in judicial decision support systems. The framework integrates a hybrid transformer-based architecture within a structured two-stage pipeline, enabling both bias classification and multi-class verdict prediction on real-world legal case data.

A key contribution of NEXAJudicia is the introduction of the Bias Attribution Index (BAI), a statistical metric that quantifies the influence of sensitive attributes on model predictions. Empirical results demonstrate consistently low bias levels (BAI < 0.01), ensuring ethically aligned and fair decision-making.

The system further incorporates Attribution-aware Retrieval-Augmented Generation (A-RAG) and explainability mechanisms to provide detailed, evidence-based justifications for predicted outcomes. This ensures that predictions are driven by legally relevant facts and contextual information rather than demographic or sensitive features.

With an achieved performance of ~81–83% accuracy in verdict prediction, NEXAJudicia highlights both the potential and inherent challenges of AI in the legal domain, where uncertainty is largely influenced by legal ambiguity and judicial discretion.

🚀 Objective: To develop a transparent, interpretable, and bias-audited AI system that supports judicial decision-making without replacing human judgment.
